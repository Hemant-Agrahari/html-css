FAQs

A water softener removes minerals like calcium and magnesium from hard water through a process called ion exchange. Hard water minerals are replaced with sodium or potassium ions, resulting in softened water that is gentler on your plumbing, appliances, and skin.

Signs of hard water include scale buildup on faucets and showerheads, spots on dishes and glassware, dry or itchy skin after bathing, and reduced efficiency of soaps and detergents. A water hardness test can confirm if a softener is needed.

Most water softeners require servicing once a year. Regular maintenance includes refilling the salt, cleaning the brine tank, and checking settings to ensure optimal performance. Our team can schedule routine maintenance visits for you.

With proper maintenance, a high-quality water softener can last between 10 to 20 years. The lifespan depends on the type of system, water quality in your area, and how well the system is maintained.

If your water softener is leaking, turn off the water supply to the unit immediately and contact a professional. Do not attempt to repair it yourself, as improper handling can worsen the damage. Our team is available 24/7 for emergency repairs.
