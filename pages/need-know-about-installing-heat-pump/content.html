What Should You Know About The Working Process?

A heat pump transfers the energy from one source to another. The heat pumps pull the outside air during the winter and transfer hot air inside. The machine cycles heat into the house until it makes your home comfortable.

Similarly, it simply reverses the working process in summers and cools down your home by extracting the heat. It operates both heating and cooling systems. A heat pump contains a few parts: outdoor units, refrigerant, indoor unit, reversing valve, compressor, and expansion valve. Different parts have different functionalities and make the system efficient.

What Should A Person Look For Before Buying A Heat Pump?

  • Zone-controlled Heating: Buy the heat pumps with the zone-controlled heating feature. By doing so, you can save your utility bills. Apart from that, it gives you comfort. Because of this feature, the heat pump delivers the air in a more controlled and slower way.
  • BTU & COP Capacity: You should consider a few factors when shopping for the heat pump. These factors include the window, ceiling height, door size, and window.   • Installing Condenser Unit: The condenser unit is the part of the heat pump located outside. Before installing, make sure you have cleaned the areas of dirt. It should be beyond the roof's drip edge; otherwise, it could fall onto the unit and decrease the performance.
  • The Air Handling Mounts: The expert heating contractor in San Diego installs indoor air handling mounts. They ensure that each air handling unit is placed 30 feet beyond the condenser. For this, the experts will drill the hole on the mounting position.
